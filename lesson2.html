What are Jama'ah prayers?

In arabic, the literal meaning of Jama'ah is 'group'. Islamically, it refers to the connection between the Imam (the one who leads a prayer) and the ma'mum (those who follow the imam). In simple terms, jama'ah prayers simply mean praying as a group, with one person leading the rest.

The ruling of Jama'ah prayers

The ruling of jama'ah prayers is fardh kifayah, or an obligation for the community. For the five daily prayers, it is imposed upon men who are capable of doing it to perform it at a mosque. This is so the community will perform every obligatory prayer as a group (jama'ah).

وعن ابن عمر رضي الله عنهما أن رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم قال‏:‏ ‏"‏صلاة الجماعة أفضل من صلاة الفذ بسبع وعشرين درجة‏.‏ ‏(‏‏(‏متفق عليه‏)‏‏)‏
The Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "Salat in congregation is twenty-seven times more meritorious than a Salat performed individually."

The Wisdom behind Jama'ah prayers

When the prophet (ﷺ) and his followers were still in Makkah, they could not practice Islam openly. Thus, it was impossible for them to hold jama'ah prayers. Once the Muslims migrated to Madinah, they were able to perform jama'ah prayers and enjoy it's benefits.

  • It encourages love among muslims, as meeting to perform prayers fosters feelings of love
  • It leads to learning more about our muslim neighbours and friends
  • It helps us meet new people, and help out newcomers to the community
  • It showcases islamic rituals and its value, for if we prayed at home by ourselves, we would not see the impact of jama'ah on the muslim community

Who is the imam?

In the words of the prophet (ﷺ):

‏ قال رسول الله صلى الله عليه وسلم ‏:‏ ‏"‏يؤم القوم أقرؤهم لكتاب الله، فإن كانوا في القراءة سواء، فأعلمهم بالسنة، فإن كانوا في السنة سواء، فأقدمهم هجرة، فإن كانوا في الهجرة سواء، فأقدمهم سنًا، ولا يؤمن الرجل الرجل في سلطانه، ولا يقعد في بيته على تكرمته إلا بإذنه‏"‏ ‏(‏‏(‏رواه مسلم‏)‏‏)‏‏.‏
Messenger of Allah (ﷺ) said, "The person who is best versed in the recitation of the Book of Allah, should lead the prayer; but if all those present are equally versed in it, then the one who has most knowledge of the Sunnah; if they are equal in that respect too, then the one who has emigrated (to Al-Madinah) first, if they are equal in this respect also, then the oldest of them. No man should lead another in prayer where the latter has authority, or sit in his house, without his permission"

From the hadith, we know that whoever leads the prayer must be the most proficent in reading the quran, followed by islamic knowledge, followed by age. From this, we can tell that becoming an imam is a big responsiblity.

What are Jumu'ah prayers?

Jumu'ah prayers are the best of all fardh prayers. It is comprised of two rakaat performed during zhuhr time on fridays.

Ruling
Wajib, or Compulsory for those who fulfill all requirents to perform it.

It is stated in the quran:

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا إِذَا نُودِيَ لِلصَّلَاةِ مِنْ يَوْمِ الْجُمُعَةِ فَاسْعَوْا إِلَىٰ ذِكْرِ اللَّهِ وَذَرُوا الْبَيْعَ ۚ ذَٰلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَكُمْ إِنْ كُنْتُمْ تَعْلَمُونَ
O you who have believed, when [the adhan] is called for the prayer on the day of Jumu'ah [Friday], then proceed to the remembrance of Allah and leave trade. That is better for you, if you only knew.

The virtues of Jumu'ah prayers

وعنه عن النبي، صلى الله عليه وسلم قال‏:‏ ‏ "‏الصلوات الخمس والجمعة إلى الجمعة، ورمضان إلى رمضان، مكفرات ما بينهن إذا اجتنبت الكبائر‏"‏ ‏(‏‏(‏رواه مسلم‏)‏‏)‏‏.‏
The Prophet (ﷺ) said, "The five daily (prescribed) Salat, and Friday (prayer) to the next Friday (prayer), and the fasting of Ramadan to the next Ramadan, is expiation of the sins committed in between them, so long as major sins are avoided."

The hadith shows us that jumu'ah prayers absolve us of our minor sins, and gives us a clean slate until the next friday prayers. Aside from social benefits of gathering every week and meeting up with each other, muslims can also benefit spiritually by performing jumu'ah prayers.

Rules on ruling

It is important to note that jumu'ah prayers are wajib (compulsory) only if one fulfills ALL of the following criteria:

  1. Islam, thus it is not obligated for non-muslims
  2. Puberty, thus it is not obligated for babies and young children. however, those who have reached the age of differentiating right from wrong are encouraged to perform it.
  3. Presence of mind, thus it is not obligated for those who are insane
  4. Male, thus it is not obligated for women
  5. Health, thus it is not obligated for the sick who are incapable of attending
  6. Freedom, thus it is not obligatory for slaves
  7. Residence, thus it is not obligatory for travellers

Obligations of Jumu'ah prayers

To ensure jumu'ah prayers are done correctly, the following rules must be fulfilled:
  1. To take place in a location that muslims are knwon to gather in
  2. To have a group of at least 40 men
  3. To take place during zhuhr time
  4. As much as possible, to not have multiple groups of people praying jumu'ah in the same place (to only have one big group)

The Ettiquettes of Jumu'ah prayers

There are many ettiquettes and sunnah that can be followed for jumu'ah prayers. The following are simply a handful of them:

  • To bathe on friday morning
  • To cleanse and perfume onesself
  • To wear the best of clothes, preferebly in white
  • To recite du'as on the way to the mosque
  • To walk to the mosque in a calm and collected manner
  • To read surah al Kahf
  • To increase wishing peace and blessing upong the prophet (ﷺ)
  • To pay attention to the sermons

Masbuq for Jumu'ah

What happens if you are late to jumu'ah prayers? How late do you have to be to miss it? If you arrive late and start praying when the imam has already began his prayers, your prayer is called masbuq But don't panic! You still have a chance of getting your jumu'ah prayers accepted!

  • If you are late and start the prayer from the ruku' of the first rakaat, your prayer is valid
  • If you are late and start your prayer before the ruku' of the second rakaat your prayer is valid, and you have to make up for the lost rakaat after the imam has finished
  • If you are late and start after the ruku' of the second rakaat, youre jumu'ah prayer is not valid and you will have to make up for it by praying zhuhr after the imam has finished praying

As long as you catch the last rakaat of the prayer, you have got it.

The Prophet (ﷺ) said: "Whoever catches up with a rak'ah of jumu'ah prayer has caught up with it."
