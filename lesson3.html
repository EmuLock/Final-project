What is Solat Musafir?

Solat Musafiris made especially for the travellers that travel away from their country and also to make them easier to perform their prayers.

Ways to do Solat Musafir

It is divided into two: Solat Qasar (صلاة القصر) and Solat Jama' (صلاة الجمع).

1. Solat Qasar (صلاة القصر)

Solat Qasar is to shorten the numbers of rakaat in certain prayers.

It is only applicable to the 4 rakaat prayers. Therefore, you can shorten the rakaat for Zohor, Asar and Isyak to 2 rakaat.

# Name of prayer Number Of Rakaat Qasar
1 Fajr 2 X
2 Zohor 4 2
3 Asar 4 2
4 Maghrib 3 X
5 Isyak 4 2

2. Solat Jama' (صلاة الجمع)

It is to combine different prayers at one of the time. It is only applicable to Zohor, Asar, Maghrib and Isyak.

There are 2 types of Solat Jama' :

  1. You can bring the prayer earlier from their original time. (جمع تقديم)
    • Asar to Zohor
    • Isyak to Maghrib
  2. You can bring the prayer later from their original time. (جمع تأخير)
    • Zohor to Asar
    • Maghrib to Isyak

Wait!

  • You cannot combine Fajr with the prayer before or after it. Example: Isyak & Fajr or Fajr & Zohor.
  • You cannot combine Asar and Maghrib.

# Name of prayer Jama' Type of Jama'
1 Fajr X X
2 Zohor Allowed to combine with Asar جمع تقديم
3 Asar Allowed to combine with Zohor جمع تأخير
4 Maghrib Allowed to combine with Isyak جمع تقديم
5 Isyak Allowed to combine with Maghrib جمع تأخير

Conditions that allows travellers to shorten and combine their prayers.

  1. The destination must be more than 81-87km and from two points of their country.
  2. The destination must be known: the traveller must know where the destination is.
  3. The traveller should not travel for disobedience.

Conditions that allows travellers to shorten your prayers.

  1. The traveller can shorten the prayer during the journey.
  2. They must cross the border of the country.
  3. The traveller has no intention to stay more than 4 days, excluding the day the traveller arrives and departs from the country.
    • If the traveller intends to stay for more than 4 days, the traveller is considered as a resident of that country and the traveller cannot shorten the prayers but only during the journey.
    • However, if the traveller travels between multiple places in two phases or more and resides at each place for four days or less, the traveller can shorten the prayers.
        • For example, the traveller travels
      • from Singapore to KL -> 2 days
      • from Kuala Lumpur to Bangkok -> 3 days
      • from Bangkok to Jakarta -> 4 days
        • Therefore, the traveller is allowed to shorten the prayers.
  4. The traveller should not follow the imam of the country during prayers. It means that the traveller is unable to pray together with the imam in that place as the imam prays fully. If the traveller follows, it is a must for the traveller to follow the imam that prays the full rakaat.

Conditions that allow travellers to combine their prayers.

جمع تقديم جمع تأخير
Order, You have to pray Zohor then Asar and the same goes for Maghrib then Isyak. Intention to Jama' Taakhir at the time of the first prayer, the traveller should niat at Zohor that he wants to combine Zohor and Asar later. So do Maghrib at Isyak.
Niat Jama’, you have to niat at Zohor that you want to combine your Asar and Zohor prayers. So do Isyak at Maghrib. Continuation in being excused until the end of the second prayer. Excused means to travel or sick. It is not regarded as prayer outside the time.
Pray the prayers continuously. You should initiate the second prayer as soon as you had finished the first prayer and do not separate them with zikr, dua or anything else.
Continuation in being excused until "takbiratul ihram" for the second prayer. Excused means to travel or sick. For example, if the traveller prays in the train during the time he returns, but he arrives in Singapore before the "takbiratul ihram" for the second prayer, therefore he is not allowed to combine his prayer but have to pray the second prayer at its own time.

How to perform your prayers when you are sick.

If the sick is weak to stand and perform the prayers, the sick are allowed to pray while sitting, lying down on the side of the body or what the sick is capable to do. But if the sick are unable to pray at its own time, the sick are allowed to combine the prayers.

The patient should perform their prayer as much as the patient could.

  1. If the patient can stand, therefore the patient should stand to pray.
  2. If the patient can rukuk and sujud, it is recommended to do it too.
  3. If the patient can stand but too weak to rukuk or sujud, it is a must for the patient to stand and pray but a gesture to rukuk then sit and gesture to sujud.
    4. Just as what we always see at the mosque especially for the elderly.
  4. If the patient is weak to sujud and rukuk, the patient can show the gesture by lowering the head and show that sujud is lower than rukuk.
  5. If the patient cannot sujud only, the patient can do it once.
  6. If the patient cannot stand and sit, the patient is allowed to pray his prayers In the position of lying down on the side of the body.

Thus, it is clear that it is not permissible to leave a prayer in any way but it is a must for the mukallaf to take care of their prayers either they are healthy or sick. However, it is not allowed to leave prayers until the time is up or sick, as long as the person is sane.
