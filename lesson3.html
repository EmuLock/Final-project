What are Musafir prayers?

Musafir prayerswere made specially for travellers to make it easier for them to perform their prayers.

Ways to perform Musafir prayers

It is divided into two: Qasar Prayers (صلاة القصر) and Jama' Prayers (صلاة الجمع).

1. Qasar Prayers (صلاة القصر)

Qasar prayers is to shorten the numbers of rakaat in certain prayers.

It is only applicable to 4 rakaat prayers. Therefore, you can only shorten the rakaat for Zohor, Asar and Isyak to 2 rakaat.

# Name of prayer Number Of Rakaat Qasar
1 Fajr 2 X
2 Zohor 4 2
3 Asar 4 2
4 Maghrib 3 X
5 Isyak 4 2

2.Jama' Prayers (صلاة الجمع)

It is to combine two different prayers and perform both at once. It is only applicable to Zohor, Asar, Maghrib and Isyak.

There are 2 types of Jama' Prayers :

  1. You can bring the prayer back from its original time, which is Jama' Taqdim.(جمع تقديم)
    • Asar to Zohor
    • Isyak to Maghrib
  2. You can bring the prayer forward, which is Jama' Ta'khir . (جمع تأخير)
    • Zohor to Asar
    • Maghrib to Isyak

Wait!

  • You cannot combine Fajr with the prayer before or after it. Example: Isyak & Fajr or Fajr & Zohor.
  • You cannot combine Asar and Maghrib.

# Name of prayer Jama' Type of Jama'
1 Fajr X X
2 Zohor Allowed to combine with Asar جمع تقديم
3 Asar Allowed to combine with Zohor جمع تأخير
4 Maghrib Allowed to combine with Isyak جمع تقديم
5 Isyak Allowed to combine with Maghrib جمع تأخير

Conditions that allows travellers to shorten and combine their prayers.

  1. The destination must be more than 81-87km and from two points of their country.
  2. The destination must be known: the traveller must know where the destination is.
  3. The traveller should not travel for the sake of disobedience.

Conditions that allow travellers to shorten their prayers.

  1. The traveller can shorten the prayer during the journey.
  2. They must cross the border of the country.
  3. The traveller has no intention to stay more than 4 days, excluding the day the traveller arrives and departs from the country.
    • If the traveller intends to stay for more than 4 days, he is considered a resident of the country and cannot shorten the prayers outside of his journey.
    • However, if the traveller travels between multiple places in two phases or more and resides at each place for four days or less, he can shorten the prayers.
        • For example, if the traveller travels
      • from Singapore to KL -> 2 days
      • from Kuala Lumpur to Bangkok -> 3 days
      • from Bangkok to Jakarta -> 4 days
        • In this scenario, the traveller is allowed to shorten the prayers.
  4. The traveller should not follow the imam of the country during his prayers as the imam will not be praying Jama' or Qasar. If the traveller decides to follow the imam, he must pray the full prayer

Conditions that allow travellers to combine their prayers.

جمع تقديم جمع تأخير
Order : You have to pray Zohor then Asar and the same goes for Maghrib then Isyak. Intention to Jama' Taakhir at the time of the first prayer, the traveller should niat at Zohor that he wants to combine Zohor and Asar later. So do Maghrib at Isyak.
Intention to Jama’: you must have the intention at Zohor that you want to combine your Asar and Zohor prayers, as with Isya' and Maghrib. Continuation in being excused until the end of the second prayer. Excused means to travel or to be sick.
Pray the prayers continuously. You should initiate the second prayer as soon as you have finished the first prayer and must not separate them with zikr, dua or anything else.
Continuation in being excused until "takbiratul ihram" for the second prayer. For example, if the traveller prays in the train during the time he returns, but he arrives in Singapore before the "takbiratul ihram" for the second prayer, therefore he is not allowed to combine his prayer but have to pray the second prayer at its own time.

How to perform your prayers when you are sick.

If you are too ill to stand and perform the prayers, you are allowed to pray while sitting, lying down on the side of the body or whatever you are capable of doing. If you are unable to pray at your own time, the you are allowed to combine the prayers.

A patient must perform their prayers as much as they are capable.

  1. If the patient can stand, the patient should stand to pray.
  2. If the patient can ruku' and sujud,he should do so.
  3. If the patient can stand but is too weak to ruku' or sujud, he must stand and pray but perform a gesture to ruku' then sit and bow to sujud.
    4. This can often be seen at the mosque especially for the elderly.
  4. If the patient is too weak to sujud and ruku', the patient can show the gesture by lowering his head and show that a sujud is lower than rukuk.
  5. If the patient cannot sujud only, the patient can do it once.
  6. If the patient cannot stand and sit, the patient is allowed to pray while lying down.

Thus, it is clear that it is not permissible to disregard one's prayers regardless of where or not one is healthy or sick.
